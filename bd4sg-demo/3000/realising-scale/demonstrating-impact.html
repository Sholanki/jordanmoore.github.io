Demonstrating Impact

Mobile data-driven analytics and solutions have a wide range of applications.From mitigating the effects of climate change, to informing policy decisions during disease outbreaks, the value can be demonstrated for a broad cross-section of humanitarian issues.

For example:

  • Improved health of the population through disease containment and better targeting of healthcare resources.
  • protecting the quality of life for those displaced (or at risk of displacement) from their homes - whether that be as the result of a sudden crisis or a long-term change, such as that driven by climate change.
  • save lives by supporting rapid decision-making and response – for instance in the event of a destructive disaster, or an outbreak of a disease.

In addition, these initiatives can provide other value-add to governments by such as :

  • More efficient use of budgets – data-derived insights can be used to make more effective decisions about the allocation of resources.
  • More effective plans – governments and agencies can use data to create predictive models to mitigate risk, as well as react to it, in the event of disasters and diseases, for example.
  • Improved ability to measure impact and demonstrate results – when decision making is based upon data-derived insights, that data can be built into measurable impact reports

The Big Data for Social Good programme has examined in detail several applications of mobile analytics, which are captured as case studies in this toolkit. (link to case studies section)
