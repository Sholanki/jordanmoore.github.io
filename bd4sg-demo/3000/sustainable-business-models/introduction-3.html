Introduction

Mobile big data implementations have proven their proficiency as a decision support tool for governments and humanitarian agencies, transforming how they respond to the most pressing humanitarian issues of our time. Our case studies demonstrate how the insights delivered can improve policy planning and accelerate response efforts, ultimately saving lives. Now the question is: how can we create the right environment for mobile big data solutions to thrive sustainably? Meeting this challenge will require a long-term strategy that embeds investment, resources, skills acquisition and feedback mechanisms that aren’t feasible for one-off initiatives. To deliver scale and maximise social benefit, there is a need to move from promising one-off projects to a fully operational production service where insights are delivered on a continuous, efficient and predictable basis. The adoption of a sustainable business model at the right point in the lifecycle of a mobile big data initiative is a key enabler to realise this objective.

In a new report, GSMAi researched the fundamental components that drive sustainability in mobile big data services and explored the types of business models that that are most relevant to mobile big data applications
